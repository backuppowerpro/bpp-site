FAQ

Common questions

Does my generator need a 240V outlet?

For most whole-panel hookups, yes. A 240V outlet, usually an L14-30 on portables in the 5,000 watt range or larger, lets the interlock power both legs of your panel. Send me the model when you request your quote and I will confirm it fits before we schedule.

How long until I'm scheduled?

We pull the permit the same day you approve, and installs are typically on the calendar within a week. Processing time depends on the local jurisdiction, so plan on a 7 to 14 day window start to finish.

Do I need Duke Energy permission?

No. A mechanical interlock is a code-approved transfer method that never backfeeds the grid, so there is no utility application or Duke Energy sign-off needed. The county permit and inspection cover it.

Are 50A installs more expensive than 30A?

Slightly, the inlet box and breaker are both larger. The final number depends mostly on the run length from your generator's spot to your panel, we'll give you the exact price after a quick look at your setup.

Do you service Campobello, Chesnee, and Landrum?

Yes, all Spartanburg County. Same process, same price (minus any extra-long-run surcharge if your panel is 75+ feet from the inlet).
