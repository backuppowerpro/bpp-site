Carbon Monoxide, Non-Negotiable
Never run your generator inside your home, garage, shed, or any covered area, even with the door open. Keep it at least 20 feet from every window, door, and vent. Point the exhaust away from the house. CO is colorless and odorless and kills before you realize anything is wrong. This is your responsibility, not ours. Install a CO detector on every level of your home.
⚡
Compatible Generator Outlets
Your inlet accepts generators with a 240V twist-lock outlet, either NEMA L14-30 (30-amp, 4-prong) or NEMA L14-50 (50-amp, 4-prong). Do not connect any other plug type. Most 5,000-12,000 watt portable generators use the L14-30. Check your generator's spec label or owner's manual to confirm. Questions? Call us at (864) 863-7800 before connecting.
Your System
What was installed and how it works
An inlet box was mounted on the exterior of your home and wired directly to your electrical panel. An interlock kit was installed on your panel, a physical sliding device that makes it mechanically impossible for your main breaker and generator breaker to be on at the same time. This protects utility workers from backfeed and protects your home. Because the interlock is physical, not electronic, you cannot accidentally have both power sources connected at once, the mechanism simply won't allow it. When you want to use your generator, you plug a power cord from the generator into the inlet, then use the interlock to transfer your panel from grid power to generator power. Follow the procedure below every time for safe, reliable operation.
A sticker with this startup & shutdown sequence will be installed inside your panel cover at the time of installation.
Starting Up
1
Position generator outside, at least 20 ft from any window, door, or vent. Exhaust pointing away.
2
Start the generator and let it run for 60 seconds before connecting anything.
3
Plug the generator power cord into the exterior inlet box.
4
Turn off any high-draw breakers (AC, electric water heater, dryer), prevents overloading the generator the moment power transfers.
5
At your panel: slide the interlock to turn the MAIN breaker OFF.
6
Turn the GENERATOR breaker ON.
7
Turn on individual circuit breakers one at a time, not all at once. Watch your total wattage.
Shutting Down
1
Turn off individual circuit breakers one at a time until all are off.
2
Turn the GENERATOR breaker OFF.
3
Slide the interlock to turn the MAIN breaker back ON to restore grid power.
4
Go outside and unplug the power cord from the inlet box.
5
Let the generator cool for 2 minutes before refueling. Never refuel while it's running.
Always follow your generator's owner's manual. The procedures above cover operating your inlet system. Your manual has model-specific instructions for startup, fuel type, oil, runtime limits, and storage that take priority over any general guidance.

Neutral-Ground Bond, Action Required Before First Use
Most portable generators ship from the factory with the neutral and ground wires bonded together inside the generator. That's fine for extension cord use. But when your generator connects to your home's panel through this inlet, your panel already has its own neutral-ground bond. Two bond points in the same system causes GFCI outlets to trip randomly and is a code violation (NEC 250.34).

The fix is called a floating neutral, converting the generator so it no longer bonds neutral to ground internally. This is typically a removable plug or a single wire inside the generator's control panel and takes a few minutes at a small engine shop.
Signs you have a bonded neutral (needs fixing): GFCI outlets trip immediately or randomly after connecting the generator · Breakers trip for no apparent reason · Lights flicker or behave unexpectedly
How to confirm it's been fixed: Search your generator's model number + "floating neutral" for instructions · Ask any small engine shop · Backup Power Pro can verify during your walkthrough
Load Management
Don't run everything at once
Your generator has a rated running wattage, the total watts it can sustain. Motors (refrigerators, AC units, well pumps) pull 2-3× their running watts for a few seconds on startup before settling down. This is normal, but it means you cannot calculate your load by simply adding up running watts. Turn on large motor-driven appliances one at a time and wait for them to settle before turning on the next. If your generator trips during startup, the load is too high, turn something off and try again. Check your owner's manual for your generator's exact running and surge watt ratings.
Generator Maintenance (Your Responsibility)
→Run your generator under load for 30 min every 90 days to keep the fuel system clean and verify it starts before a storm.
→Add fuel stabilizer if gas will sit more than 30 days. Stale fuel is the #1 reason generators fail when needed.
→Follow your owner's manual oil change schedule. Change before each season if stored over the summer.
→Annual service by a small engine shop is recommended to inspect fuel lines, air filter, spark plug, and voltage output.

Never Do These
✕
Tamper with, remove, or bypass the interlock kit. It physically prevents backfeed to the grid, a danger to utility workers. As installed, you can't do this accidentally. Never defeat the protection.
✕
Run the generator inside, in a garage, shed, or any covered space, even briefly or with a door open.
✕
Plug appliances or extension cords directly into the inlet box, it's a connection point for the generator cord only, not an outlet.
✕
Exceed your generator's rated running wattage. Overloading trips the generator and can damage connected appliances.
✕
Allow anyone to use this system without reviewing this document first. If you sell the home, provide this to the buyer.
✕
Modify the inlet, interlock, panel wiring, or connected generator cord without a licensed electrician.
