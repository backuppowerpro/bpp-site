Sizing the inlet

Two sizes cover almost every home

Match the inlet to your generator's highest-output twist-lock outlet. You'll know by looking at your generator's own panel.

30-Amp inlet

NEMA L14-30P

For generators up to about 7,500 watts running. The common size for portables in the 5 to 7kW range, four-prong twist-lock, 240V split-phase.

Powers: fridge, lights, WiFi, gas furnace blower, a smaller well pump, a few outlets.

50-Amp inlet

NEMA 14-50P / CS6375

For generators 10,000 watts running or more. The common size for 10 to 12kW portables, four-prong twist-lock, 240V split-phase with neutral.

Powers: everything the 30A does, plus larger well pumps, an electric water heater on reduced draw, and smaller central air units.

Placement

Where we mount the inlet box

Code requires the inlet outside the home, reachable by your generator cord, and protected from direct weather. We'll confirm the spot during your quote visit.

Exterior wall near your panel

The most common spot. Shortest conduit run, the least expensive install.

Garage or attached carport exterior

Works well if your panel is already in the garage, and keeps the generator covered.

Deck or patio wall

A sheltered plug-in spot when the panel itself is interior. Adds a longer conduit run.

Freestanding post

Rare, but possible. Adds cost for the post and underground conduit.

FAQ

Common questions

What if my generator has both a 30A and a 50A outlet?

Most 10 to 12kW portables offer both. We install the 50A inlet if you plan to pull more than about 7kW during outages, otherwise the 30A keeps the install at the lower end of the range. We'll confirm which fits your setup during the quote visit.

Do I need a specific cord?

Yes, one rated for your generator's output and long enough to reach the inlet. 25 ft is typical, 50 ft max. The cord cost is included if your generator does not come with one.

Can I upgrade from 30A to 50A later?

Yes. Re-doing an inlet costs less than the first install, because the conduit and panel work is already done. Easier to size it right up front if you think you will upgrade your generator.

Does the inlet work with any generator?

Only generators with a matching 240V twist-lock outlet, NEMA L14-30 for 30A inlets, 14-50 or CS6364 for 50A. If your generator is 120V only, it is not compatible. Upgrade to a 240V portable, then book.

Does the fuel type matter?

No. The inlet does not care what the generator burns, gas, diesel, or dual-fuel. As long as the output outlet matches, any fuel works. Check your town on our list of cities for local timelines.
