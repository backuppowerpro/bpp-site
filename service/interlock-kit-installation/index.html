How it works

A physical lockout, not a promise

The interlock is a small metal plate that slides between your main breaker and a dedicated generator breaker. When the main is on, the generator breaker is physically blocked. When the main is off, the generator breaker slides free. It is impossible to have both on at once, which is exactly what NEC code requires to prevent backfeeding the grid.

Why this matters

Backfeeding the grid during an outage can injure a line crew working to restore power, damage your equipment when the grid comes back, and lead a utility to refuse to re-energize a house it suspects has uncontrolled backfeed. The interlock removes that risk entirely.

Compatibility

Panels we install interlock kits on

Interlocks are panel-brand specific, a Square D kit doesn't fit a Siemens panel. We stock kits for the common brands in the Upstate.

FAQ

Common questions

Can I install an interlock myself?

SC code requires licensed electrician work for panel modifications. The interlock kit is sold at hardware stores, but pulling the permit and passing inspection requires a licensed contractor. DIY installs that fail inspection cost more to fix than having us do it right the first time.

How long does an interlock last?

The mechanical plate is good for the life of the panel. Cycling it on and off does not wear it out in any practical sense.

What if I move?

The interlock stays with the house. It is a code-compliant home upgrade, and appraisers and home inspectors generally view it favorably. Check your town on our list of cities for local timelines.
