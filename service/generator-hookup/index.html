Inlet box

Weatherproof conductors

Mechanical interlock

A code-approved slide that makes it physically impossible to run the utility and your generator at the same time, safe for you and the line crews.

FAQ

Common questions

Does my generator need a 240V outlet?

Yes. A 240V outlet, usually an L14-30 on portables in the 5,000 watt range or larger, lets the interlock power both legs of your panel. Send me the model with your quote and I will confirm it before we schedule.

How long does the install take?

The inlet plus interlock is a one day job on site. Start to finish, including the permit and inspection, most homes are done within a week to two weeks depending on how fast the county schedules the inspection.

Do I need a permit?

Yes, and it is included. Any generator hookup to your panel is electrical work that requires a permit and a county inspection. We pull the permit under SC License 2942 and handle the inspection, both are in the price.

What size generator do I need?

Most homes are well covered by a portable in the 5,000 to 7,500 watt range, which runs the fridge, well pump, heat, and lights together. Tell me what you want to keep on and I will tell you honestly whether your generator handles it.
