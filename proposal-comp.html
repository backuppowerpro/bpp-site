Generator Operating & Safety Notice
🔌 Generator bonding (floating neutral), do this first
Most portable generators ship with the neutral and ground bonded together inside. Once yours connects to your panel (which already has its own bond), that double bond trips GFCIs and is a code violation (NEC 250.34). Convert your generator to a floating neutral before first use. A small engine shop can do it in minutes. This is the owner's responsibility.
⚠️ Carbon monoxide
Run the generator outdoors only, at least 20 feet from windows, doors, and vents, with the exhaust pointed away. Never in a garage or covered space, even with the door open. CO is odorless and deadly. Install CO detectors on every level of your home.
⚡ Compatible generators only
Connect only a 240V twist-lock generator (NEMA L14-30 or L14-50). Do not connect any other plug type.
🔁 Operate in sequence
Start the generator, plug into the connection box, slide the interlock (main off, generator on), then switch on circuits one at a time within your wattage. Reverse the steps to shut down, and let it cool before refueling.
🛠️ What we are responsible for
Our installation covers the connection box, the interlock, and the wiring between them, permitted and inspected. It does not include your generator, its maintenance, the neutral-ground bond, or anything modified after we leave. You are responsible for safe operation per these instructions.
