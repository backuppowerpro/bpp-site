Why online quotes vary so much

The spread is real, and it is mostly about what each quote leaves out:

  • Labor-only or travel-only quotes. A low number is often just the visit, with parts, permit, and inspection billed on top.
  • Materials-only estimates. The hardware is priced by itself, before anyone installs it.
  • The quotes that skip the permit. The cheapest offers usually do not pull a permit or schedule an inspection. That saves them money and leaves you with an unpermitted electrical change.

What actually moves the price

To land on your exact number, here is what I look at for your specific setup:

  • Your panel. Age and available breaker space. A full panel may need a slot freed up first.
  • The run. How far the inlet box sits from the panel, and whether the panel is indoors or outdoors.
  • 30A vs 50A. Matched to your generator's outlet. The 50A path costs a little more but future-proofs a larger generator.
The hidden cost of the cheap route: an unpermitted install, or a back-fed "suicide cord," is not just unsafe. It can void a homeowner's insurance claim and get flagged during a home sale inspection. The permitted inlet and interlock is the version that holds up. Honest, not fear, it is simply the difference between a job that passes inspection and one that does not.

This connects the portable generator you already own to power your selected essential circuits through your panel, the fridge, the furnace blower, the well pump, some lights, the internet.
