Why Your Generator Is Only Half the Solution

You bought a generator. Smart move. But right now it's sitting in your garage waiting for a storm. When that storm hits at 2 AM, what's your actual plan?

Run extension cords through a cracked window? That gets you a fridge and a lamp. Maybe your phone charger. That's it.

What extension cords can't power: Your well pump. Your HVAC. Your sump pump. Anything on a 240-volt circuit.

The real solution: connect your generator directly to your breaker panel through a code-compliant inlet and interlock system. One cord. Full panel access. Done in a morning.

You already spent $800 to $2,000 on that generator. It's 90% of the way there. The inlet and interlock connection is the last piece that makes it actually work the way you imagined when you bought it.

The Permit Question

"Do I really need a permit?" Yes. And here's why that's a good thing.

  • Insurance protection. Unpermitted electrical work can void your claim.
  • Home resale. Unpermitted work shows up on inspections.
  • Safety check. County inspector confirms everything is right.
You don't deal with any of it. We pull the permit, schedule the inspection, handle the paperwork. All included.
